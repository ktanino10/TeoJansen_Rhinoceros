# Assembly and Learning Guide

This guide will help you understand and build your Theo Jansen Rhinoceros kinetic sculpture.

## Understanding the Mechanism

### The Theo Jansen Linkage

The Theo Jansen mechanism is a sophisticated linkage system that converts rotary motion into a smooth walking motion. The key features include:

- **Multiple Link System**: Connected bars of specific lengths create the walking motion
- **Critical Ratios**: The length ratios between links are crucial for smooth movement
- **Single Drive Point**: One rotating crank drives the entire leg mechanism
- **Elegant Simplicity**: Complex motion from simple components

### Learning Resources

Before building, it's helpful to understand:

1. **Basic Mechanics**: How linkages work
2. **Rotary to Linear Motion**: Converting circular motion to walking
3. **Kinematic Analysis**: Understanding the path traced by the foot

**Recommended Resources:**

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Leg doesn't move smoothly**
- Check that all joints can rotate freely
- Verify link lengths match the design
- Ensure no parts are binding

**Parts don't fit together**
- Check your 3D printer calibration
- May need to scale parts slightly
- Sand down tight-fitting areas

**Mechanism doesn't walk**
- Verify all link connections are correct
- Check that the crank is properly attached
- Ensure legs are properly phased

1. **Observation**: Watch how the mechanism moves
2. **Drawing**: Sketch the linkage system
3. **Measurement**: Measure link lengths and angles
4. **Prediction**: Predict how changes affect motion
5. **Design**: Create variations of the design

### For Advanced Study

1. **Kinematic Analysis**: Calculate the foot path mathematically
2. **Optimization**: Find ideal link length ratios
3. **Simulation**: Model the mechanism in CAD software
4. **Comparison**: Study other walking mechanisms
